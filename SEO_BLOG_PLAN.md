## Keyword Strategy Overview

KiteHR competes in a space dominated by Greenhouse, Lever, and Workable — but has a decisive differentiator: a genuinely free, unlimited product. The 30-day content strategy exploits three angles simultaneously:

**Angle 1 — Cost/Free Intent Capture**
A large share of ATS searches include modifiers like "free," "affordable," "for startups," or "small business." These are pre-qualified buyers that KiteHR directly solves for. These posts are the highest-conversion content in the plan.

**Angle 2 — Topical Authority Stack**
Google ranks sites that demonstrate deep expertise across a topic cluster. Publishing across all 7 content pillars in 30 days builds a cross-linked web of content that signals authority in the HR/recruiting space — lifting not just blog traffic but all product and landing pages too.

**Angle 3 — Template & Tool Content as Lead Magnets**
KiteHR already has a rich content library (job descriptions, interview questions, email templates, hiring guides). Blog posts that reference and link into this library drive time-on-site, increase internal PageRank flow to the library, and create natural pathways to product adoption.

## Writing Style Guidelines (Apply to Every Post)

- **Voice:** Practical, direct, no HR buzzwords. The reader is a founder, hiring manager, or office manager — not a CHRO. Write like a knowledgeable colleague, not a consultant billing by the word.
- **KiteHR Mentions:** Never forced. Each post's feature tie-in is a natural 1–2 paragraph mention in context — not a sales pitch. The reader should feel like they discovered it.
- **SEO Formatting:**
  - Primary keyword in H1, first 100 words, and at least two H2s
  - Meta description: 150–160 characters, include primary keyword and a clear value statement
  - Internal links: minimum 3 per post, using natural anchor text (not "click here")
- **Word Count:** Follow each post's target. Longer posts (2,000–2,500 words) are for high-competition informational queries; shorter posts (1,400–1,800 words) for more specific or commercial queries.
